# Market Log — 3 July 2026

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**

> Within the UK one major driver of imbalance price (particularly against N2EX day ahead) is the wind outturn variance. Solar shape within day is forecastable and may not vary much to forecast, so can be observed and predicted days, months, and crudely years in advance, but the wind will only be observable within 1 or 2 weeks. 
> 
> Picking up the BMRS wind generation forecast and outturn for July 3rd, there was a continuous over-forecast of wind, on average forecasting 7.8GW and outturn was at 5.7GW. Thus, I would expect that system prices would be slightly above N2EX prices. Baseload across the entire day, the system prices were +10% more expensive than the day ahead prices (£96 vs £104), albeit with random noise within it that incorporates the balancing mechanism at work to balance the grid with the NIV mechanism. 
>
> As all CfD, which is mostly off-shore wind, is settled at day ahead reference prices (the IMRP is blended from EPEX and N2EX, [here](https://www.lowcarboncontracts.uk/news/how-intermittent-market-reference-price-imrp-will-be-calculated-from-1-january-2021/)), all CfD generators have been set up to sell volumes at day ahead, not intraday. Thus, the intra-day market becomes a major balancing act from the misforecast from day ahead, as my logic above was aligned with.  
>
> Behaviour that may be interesting to look at, independently, is the behaviour of CfD sites based on the positive IMRP vs negative system pricing events and whether the suspension of payments in negative events do trigger curtailment in one way or another.
