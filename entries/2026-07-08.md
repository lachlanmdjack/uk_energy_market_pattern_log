# Market Log — 8 July 2026

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**

> Based on overnight missile strikes from the US into Iranian land, my expectation was for natural gas prices this morning to have a significant jump in price, back to prices seen right before the Memorandum of Understanding was signed in mid-June. So far at 10am, prices have increased ~5% (having increased 5% on the 7th) to £117 for 1,000 Therms via ICE's UK NBP gas futures in August. When the MoU was announced, the future prices were £120, then dipping to £97. My expectation is therefore that prices rise another few pounds throughout the course of today as more analysis and commentary is conducted on the current situation.
>

**Secondary observation:**

> Wind forecast compared to generation for the 8th was accurate, and net interconnector flow was typical for the last few days. Not being a weekend or having excess wind on the system, system prices did not collapse beyond £80, and EPEX had a strong performance in 'predicting' these prices.
