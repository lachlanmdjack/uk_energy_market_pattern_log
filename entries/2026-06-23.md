# Market Log — 23 June 2026

--

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**
> Over the last 2 days of heat in the UK, the post-sunset demand from (baseload-shaped) airconditioning in europe has flipped interconnectors at night (as Germany/Spain are relatively more dependent on solar output), causing SSP in the UK to jump from £90 in SP37 to £173 and £80 in SP32 to £172 the next half hour (Monday had thunderstorms to solar output dropped much earlier than usual for the day after a summer solstice). 
> 
> Looking at the claude-coded 'Net_Interconnectors' graphing from these two days, the UK goes from importing +7.7GW from interconnectors to +1.5GW on Sunday, and +6.6GW to exporting -2GW overnight (3pm starts the ramp up in exporting). 

**Secondary observation:**
> Interconnectors during middle of the way may be pushing prices up (preventing negative intraday pricing in the UK) as French nuclear plants are only functioning at 61% (energygraph). High heat and low French rivers restrict nuclear operations, possibly requiring more export/demand in the UK than would otherwise be. Despite this Elexon BMRS shows the IFA/IFA2/Eleclink connectors remains relatively strong as an importer into the UK, with no export over the last week. 

> That being said, NEMO (Belgium), Viking (Denmark) and BritNed (Netherlands) have been demonstrating extreme within day shape, flipping from +3.5GW during middle of the days to -3.5GW at nights. This is a pattern that has held for recent history, but UK exports have increased slightly in frequency recently. 

**Third observation:**
> Written day after: Peak prices reached £770 for 2 full hours, having provided a fantastic arbitrage opportunity for BESS and storage hydro facilities during this period, charging with low midday prices and discharging at spill prices to capture this gain. 
>
> Elexon's REMIT dataset shows on 23/06/26, 18:30:00 that T_CDCL went 100% offline, which is Cottam Development Centre, a 400MW Combined Cycle Gas Turbine (CCGT), potentially requiring other, more expensive, gas turbines to replace this generation.  
>
> Finally, there was a power outage in Northern France reported at 9pm this day from france24. IFA started exporting another 300MW over this span, which could have eased prices slightly (instead of being consumed in France).
