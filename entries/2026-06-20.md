## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**
> Day ahead power increasing by 1.2% (purely energy), while DA gas has moved by 2.7%. As month ahead gas has not moved (0.04%), as the cost of carbon has also dramatically increased (£55.03/tCO2 £59.21), thus the (clean) spark has nararowed as follows:
> 
> Clean Spark Spread = Power price - Gas price / Efficiency - (Carbon price * Efficiency)
> 
> $CSS_{start}$ = £97.4/MWh - 99.6p/Th / 49.13% - (£55.03/tCO2 * 0.394 MWh/tCO2)
> 
> 99.6p/Th --(1 Th = 0.0293071 MWh)--> 99.6 / 0.0293071  = 3398p/MWh = £33.98/MWh
> 
> $CSS_{start}$ = £97.4/MWh - £33.98/MWh / 49.13% - £21.68/MWh
>
> $CSS_{start}$ = £6.56/MWh
> 
> $CSS_{end}$ = £98.60/MWh - 102.29p/Th / 49.13% - (£59.21/tCO2 * 0.394 MWh/tCO2) = £98.60/MWh - £71.04/MWh - £23.33/MWh
> 
> $CSS_{end}$ = £4.23/MWh
> 
> The clean spark narrowed by £2.33/MWh, most of which was the £1.65/MWh increase in cost of carbon today. 
> 
> > One observation is that the impact in the middle east (increasing gas prices today due to uncertainty over the weekend) did not increase spot power prices as much as it would have, without the carbon price increase.
> 
> The potential cause for this is increased visibility caused by the UK-European Trading Scheme interlinking plans (https://gmk.center/en/news/european-carbon-prices-rose-to-e80-t-in-june/). The cost of importing natural gas into the UK will increase.
> 
> An impact of narrowing clean spark spread shifts margin away from gas-powered plants, especially low-efficiency plants may be less responsive to pricing signals in cases of tight power market, making imbalance market more volatile.
