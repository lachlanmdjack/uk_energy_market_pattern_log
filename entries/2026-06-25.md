# Market Log — 25 June 2026

--

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**
> Wind generation has started increasing today, with the combination of Solar + Wind reaching it's peak output today at 20GW (out of nearly 40GW of demand at its midday peak). Today's SSP did not show this pattern strongly, fluctuating between £120 and £80 for the majority of the day. Potentially this is because gas demand still held at 6GW throughout the entire midday. 
> 
> As solar output diminished throughout the country, wind generation has climbed from 4GW in the morning to 14GW at the time of writing of 9pm. This has held the system price steady at £100, having one period at £150, minimising the requirement for CCGT and other gas turbines pushing the imbalance price. As wind increases tonight, I expect prices to finally fall throughout the night, however due to the use of airconditioning/refrigeration/fans in the UK and London into tomorrow, for it to not collapse completely. 
> 
> Referencing with Elexon's public wind generation forecast, wind generation will peak at 11pm then fall again tomorrow, which should prevent price falling like observed yesterday. Tomorrow's peak forecast is half of today, which opens up the possibility for more high peak prices again.

**Second observation:**
> Monitoring natural gas prices for UK NBP, month ahead prices have been consistent this week, remaining within a £5 range. BBC articles have stated multiple cases of ambiguity and attacks within the strait of Hormuz this week, with evacuations being required for sailors that have been stranded. Due to attacks by unknown projectiles, there remains caution on whether the Strait can reopen in full in coming days and weeks, meaning gas prices will still be factoring this risk within it. 
> 
> This is particularly concerning for European gas storage levels. This remains near the lowest at this time of year over the last 5 years. Lower prices could promote increased injection into storage for the winter period.

**Third observation:**
> Via LinkedIn I was suggested a post, linked below, commenting on a concept I wrote about yesterday, where peak prices spiked not just due to solar ramp down but the initialization of gas plants. Pointing out that some European countries operate on 15 minute blocks, which emphasises those valuable minutes where gas turbines ramp up generation, with the highest LCOE of the operation cycle which isn't blended across an entire hour anymore, which can provide massive arbitrage opportunities for Hydro and BESS solutions, if this 15 minute window can be identified and discharged accordingly. 
> 
> "A CCGT, in the end, isn't selling electricity into that window. It's selling ramp."
