# Market Log — 27 June 2026 and 28 June 2026

--

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**
> Saturday saw prices reaching -£1.03/MWh at midday, but only for the peak irradiance. Sunday, however, prices fell below £0 at 8am and remained at that point until 3:30pm, reaching nearly -£100 at midday. Overnights for both nights climbed to £100 according to a normal daily regime. 
> 
> When observing interconnector patterns over the weekend, I would have expected Saturday to have high levels of import and Sunady to have extraordinary levels, however, this was not what occured during the weekend. Across the 9 interconnectors, out of a possible 9.8GW of import there was 8.3GW on Saturday morning, then slowly decreasing to 5GW, 3GW and 2GW throughout the day, flipping to net export at 4:30pm. Sunday followed a similar pattern but only reached 5GW import in the morning, and started exporting at 1pm. This coincidces with the reversal of trend of negative system pricing, becoming less negative after 1pm.  
> 
> Wind generation was up to on average 11GW on Sunday, much higher than 3.5GW on Wednesday, contirbuting to the lowest daily gas usage in a while.
> 
> Both days were forecast for low prices via N2EX, achieving similar shapes where Saturday's price trough was much peakier, and Sunday was a sustained low, N2EX did not forecast heavily negative pricing.
