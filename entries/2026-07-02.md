# Market Log — 2 July 2026

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**

> As expected by Nordpool N2EX, midday prices fell to £0 and 'peaked' at 3pm at -£77. Marginal generation being renewable energy should translate very well to prices being £0, but prices were sustainably negative for the midday period (-£2 to -£15), plus the half-hour at -£80. Around most of this period the UK was net exporting via the interconnectors, likely indicating that the prices in Europe were even more negative than this from their own solar generation during the day.
> 
> Via the Energy Charts website, German prices during this time were slightly negative at -€2. This is then more likely to be NESO attempting to encourage flex opportunities to increase consumer demand, halt spill-linked and CfD contracted renewable generators, especially as the CfD payments were suspended on this day due to the extended period of negative pricing.
