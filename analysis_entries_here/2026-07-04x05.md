# Market Log — 4 July 2026 and 5 July 2026

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**

> In the UK the net importing interconnectors on July 5th reached 8GW at 5pm, an interesting situation as a few days before on the 1st at the exact same time the UK was net exporting. Observing solar output at this time for these two days in particular, there is no significant difference with 4.8GW and 5.1GW (albeit the 5th had a 20% higher peak at midday), so lack of solar demand was not a contributor for the interconnector flipping. 
> 
> Wind forecast and outturn was reasonably low at the 5th's afternoon (5GW), likely meaning the CCGT and gas turbine fleet was powering up at this time, enabling the interconnector flows to chase the spread that was being created between the multiple power markets. Generally, as shown below, the days with the higher wind days (an always-on generator, if available) has low net interconnector import, like the 2nd and 4th. Days with low wind pushes UK prices up as more UK gas turbines are required, enabling and incentivizing the interconnectors to flow to the UK and we get much more interconnector imports, like on the 3rd and 5th. 
>
> Naturally the short run marginal cost of the wind turbine regime days are much cheaper, which is how the prices on the 4th were low whereas the 5th were not, despite both being weekends with relatively similar solar output.
