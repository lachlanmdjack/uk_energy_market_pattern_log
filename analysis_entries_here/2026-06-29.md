# Market Log — 29 June 2026

--

## Observations and Drivers

**Primary observation:**
> S+1 gas prices increased on the weekend to the Monday due to uncertainties surrounding the Middle East conflict vis-a-vis claims of breaks in ceasefire and attacks on cargo ships traversing the strait. Since Friday's attacks there has been 'standing down'. Naturally prices will have been lower with the ceasefire agreement, but additional attacks like these increase prices due to increased likelihood of no near-term resolution. 
> 
> Gas prices in July have increased ~4% within day, via the above but also has moved further than the August product, possibly due to increased visibility of another UK heatwave on the horizon within the month. If wind generation produces similar to last week, i.e. still wind not being able to fill in margin demand, prices could be high again providing healthy spark spread and margin for gas turbines that will be used to fill in the additional demand due to airconditioning in the UK and mainland Europe.
